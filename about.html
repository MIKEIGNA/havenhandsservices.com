About Us

We started because
we were frustrated too

Finding someone you can trust in your home shouldn't be this hard. We built Haven Hands to make it easier for Nairobi families.

Find a Staff Our Story
How we began

It started with a bad experience

Like most Nairobi families, we'd tried hiring househelps through word of mouth, social media groups, and other agencies. It rarely ended well. Someone would steal. Someone else would just stop showing up. And the ones who were decent had never been trained on basic things like kitchen hygiene or how to use a washing machine.

So we decided to do things differently. We started Haven Hands to give families access to Staff who are actually vetted, properly trained, and treated with dignity. Because when you take care of your staff, they take care of your home.

  • We train our own Staff — not just recruit
  • Every placement comes with ongoing support
  • We treat Staff as professionals, not commodities

Why Us

What makes us different

We actually check

Background checks, reference calls, in-person interviews — we don't just take someone's word for it. We verify before we recommend.

We train them ourselves

Our Staff go through a 10-module training program — first aid, hygiene, communication, digital skills, and more. So they're ready from day one.

We don't disappear after

If things aren't working out, we'll help sort it. We do check-ins and will find a replacement if needed. That's what we're here for.

What we're about

Our goal is simple

Where we're going

We want every family in Kenya to have access to domestic help they can actually trust. No more taking chances with strangers in your home.

How we get there

By training Staff properly, treating them fairly, and being honest with families. It's not complicated — it just requires actually caring about both sides.

What we believe

The principles we actually live by

Honesty first

We tell families the truth about candidates — even when it's not what they want to hear. And we're upfront with Staff about what employers expect.

People over profit

We could place more people faster if we cut corners on vetting. We don't. A bad placement hurts everyone — the family and the Staff.

Keep improving

We review our training curriculum regularly, get feedback from families and Staff, and adjust. There's always room to do better.
